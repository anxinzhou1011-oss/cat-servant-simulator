01 / Game

Inspiration, Concepts, and Playable Experience

Inspiration

The project turns ordinary cat-care behavior into a readable interaction system: touch first, observe the response, then decide whether to groom, stop, or adjust. The fantasy is not owning a pet. It is becoming a better servant to a creature with strong opinions.

Concept

A short, repeatable grooming session where each cat has different tolerance, affection thresholds, and temporary conditions.

02 / Designs

Life Experience, Core Emotion, Core Mechanics, and Graph

Life Experience

Grooming a cat is familiar but unstable: the same gesture can be welcome, tolerated, or rejected depending on timing and place. The design uses that everyday uncertainty as the main play texture.

Core Emotion

Tender anxiety. The player wants to help, but the cat's body language and meters keep reminding them that care has limits.

Core Mechanics

  • Switch between hand and brush.
  • Touch body areas with different hidden reactions.
  • Raise satisfaction without filling anger.
  • Read expressions: normal, satisfied, angry, hiss, and sleep.
  • Handle conditions: impatient, relaxed, sleepy.

03 / Game World

Visual-Audio, Space, Cultural Layers, and Narrative Design

Visual-Audio Design

The world uses soft illustrated cats, expressive face states, handmade UI bars, cursor tools, result screens, and a small button-click sound to frame care as tactile.

Space Design

The play space is intimate and low-pressure: one cat, a few body zones, visible status feedback, and no spatial maze between the player and the animal.

Cultural Layers

The title positions the player as a servant, drawing on a shared internet-cultural joke that cats rule the household while humans negotiate for permission to help.

Narrative Design

Story is delivered through micro-reactions rather than dialogue: trust rises, patience thins, a cat hisses, or a sleepy cat accepts care and drifts off.
